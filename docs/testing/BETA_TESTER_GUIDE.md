> **Version:** 1.0
> **Updated:** March 4, 2026
> **App URL:** https://pips-app.vercel.app

---

## Getting Started

### 1. Create an Account

1. Go to https://pips-app.vercel.app
2. Click **Sign Up**
3. Enter your email and choose a password (min 8 characters)
4. Check your email and click the confirmation link
5. You'll be guided through onboarding (create your first organization)

> **Note:** If you don't receive the confirmation email, check spam. Contact Marc if the issue persists.

### 2. Create an Organization

After confirming your email, you'll be prompted to create an organization:

- **Name:** Choose any name (e.g., "My Test Org")
- This is your workspace — all projects and team members live inside it

### 3. Explore the Dashboard

Once logged in, you'll see the main dashboard with:

- **Projects** — Create and manage improvement projects
- **Tickets** — Track individual improvement items through the 6-step PIPS process
- **Knowledge Hub** — Read methodology content, guides, workbook exercises
- **Training** — Structured learning paths with quizzes and exercises
- **Workshop** — Facilitate timed workshop sessions (manager+ role)

## Key Workflows to Test

### A. Project & Ticket Lifecycle

1. Create a new project from the dashboard
2. Create a ticket inside the project
3. Walk through the PIPS steps:
   - **Step 1 (Identify):** Fill out the Problem Statement form
   - **Step 2 (Analyze):** Complete the Fishbone Diagram form
   - **Step 3 (Generate):** Do the Brainwriting exercise
   - **Step 4 (Select & Plan):** Fill the Decision Matrix
   - **Step 5 (Implement):** Create milestones and checklists
   - **Step 6 (Evaluate):** Record results and lessons learned
4. Advance the ticket through steps using the step controls

### B. Knowledge Hub

1. Navigate to **Knowledge Hub** from the sidebar
2. Browse the 4 pillars: Book, Guide, Workbook, Workshop
3. Open a book chapter — verify content renders and is readable
4. Use the search bar to find content (try "fishbone", "brainstorming")
5. Bookmark a page and check it appears in your bookmarks
6. Return to a page you previously read — scroll position should be restored

### C. Training Mode

1. Navigate to **Training** from the sidebar
2. Start a learning path
3. Complete a multiple-choice exercise
4. Write a reflection response
5. Open a practice scenario and work through the steps
6. Check the progress page to see your stats

### D. Workshop (Manager+ Only)

1. Navigate to **Knowledge Hub > Workshop**
2. Click "New Session" and choose a template
3. Start the session — try the timer (countdown + stopwatch)
4. Navigate through modules
5. Open the Participant Link in another browser/tab to see the participant view
6. Try Presentation Mode

### E. Team & Settings

1. Go to **Settings > Profile** — update your name/avatar
2. Go to **Settings > Team** — invite a team member (requires valid email)
3. Try different role assignments (member, manager, admin)
4. Check notification preferences

## What to Look For

### Usability

- Is navigation intuitive? Can you find features easily?
- Do forms have clear labels and instructions?
- Are error messages helpful when things go wrong?
- Does the app feel responsive and fast?

### Content Quality

- Is the methodology content clear and well-organized?
- Are there typos or formatting issues in the Knowledge Hub?
- Do the training exercises make sense?

### Bugs

- Any blank pages, error screens, or unexpected behavior?
- Any buttons that don't work or forms that won't submit?
- Any layout issues on your screen size?

### Performance

- How fast do pages load?
- Any noticeable delays when navigating or submitting forms?
- Does the app feel smooth on your device?

## Known Limitations

- **Email confirmation required** — new signups need to confirm email before first login
- **Single org default** — multi-org users will see their first org (no org switcher yet)
- **No password reset flow** — contact Marc if locked out
- **Workshop Realtime** — timer sync requires a stable internet connection
- **Mobile** — app is optimized for desktop; mobile layouts are basic
